Chhattisgarh - Kosa Silk saree

An eminent geographical indication, Kosa silk sarees find their home in the state of Chattisgarh.

Tracing Its Origin

Kosa silk, also known as Tussar silk, is a type of silk that originated in the Chhattisgarh districts of Champa and Korba. It is extracted from the cocoons of the antheraea mylitta silkworm, which builds its cocoons on arjun, sal, and saja trees that are native to India. The process of making Kosa silk is long and arduous, with only skilled men sorting the larvae, which are the primary source of silk. The larvae are then transferred to the trees with fresh leaves.
