Punjab - Phulkari saree

An eminent geographical indication, Phulkari saree find their home in the state of Punjab.

Tracing Its Origin

Phulkari is a traditional hand embroidery technique from Punjab, India that originated in the 15th century. It is known for its intricate floral and geometric patterns created using long and short stitches. The word Phulkari means "floral work", but the designs include not only flowers but also cover motifs and geometrical shapes

Phulkari is a traditional Punjabi embroidery that is done on the reverse side of a coarse cotton fabric, called khaddar, using colorful silk thread and a darn stitch. The word "phulkari" means "flower work" and the designs often include flowers, leaves, birds, animals, peacocks, parrots, lotus, mangoes, and paisleys. The embroidery reflects a woman's life, including weddings, births, and religious functions.
